# ARIP

**Autonomous Reliability Intelligence Platform**

The open-source Industrial Operating System for reliability intelligence, condition monitoring, digital twins, asset intelligence, and autonomous maintenance.

---

## Overview

ARIP is an open-source industrial platform designed to help organizations move from reactive and preventive maintenance toward predictive, prescriptive, and autonomous maintenance.

The project combines reliability engineering, condition monitoring, asset performance management, digital twins, industrial knowledge graphs, and explainable artificial intelligence into a unified industrial software architecture.

ARIP is currently in the early open-source architecture and prototype phase.

---

## Vision

The long-term vision of ARIP is to become a vendor-neutral Industrial Operating System for:

* Reliability Engineering
* Condition Monitoring
* Asset Performance Management
* Digital Twin Modeling
* Industrial Artificial Intelligence
* Knowledge-Based Diagnostics
* Autonomous Maintenance

The platform is designed to support heavy industries such as cement, mining, steel, oil and gas, petrochemical, power generation, water and wastewater, and transportation.

---

## Why ARIP?

Industrial maintenance and reliability software is often fragmented across separate systems for:

* Asset registers
* Condition monitoring
* CMMS / EAM
* Predictive analytics
* Failure analysis
* Digital twins
* Technical documentation
* Inspection workflows

ARIP aims to integrate these capabilities into a single open, extensible, and industry-oriented architecture.

---

## Core Capabilities

### Asset Intelligence

* Asset hierarchy modeling
* Equipment registry
* Measurement points
* Component-level structure
* Asset health scoring
* Risk profiles

### Condition Monitoring

* Vibration monitoring
* Temperature monitoring
* Thermography
* Oil analysis
* Ultrasound
* Visual inspection
* Process data analysis

### Reliability Intelligence

* Failure mode management
* FMEA / FMECA support
* Root cause analysis
* Risk-based prioritization
* Remaining useful life estimation
* Maintenance recommendation support

### Digital Twin

ARIP is designed around multiple digital twin perspectives for each asset:

* Physical Twin
* Functional Twin
* Process Twin
* Reliability Twin
* Energy Twin
* AI Twin

### Industrial Knowledge Graph

The project includes a knowledge-centric architecture for representing:

* Assets
* Components
* Failure modes
* Root causes
* Symptoms
* Maintenance actions
* Spare parts
* Historical cases

### Industrial AI

ARIP is planned to support:

* Explainable AI
* Time-series analytics
* Anomaly detection
* Fault classification
* Physics-informed models
* Causal reasoning
* Retrieval-augmented generation for technical documents

## Initial Industry Focus

The first application domain is the cement industry, especially equipment such as:

* Raw mills
* Cement mills
* Kiln drives
* Fans
* Gearboxes
* Compressors
* Pumps
* Elevators
* Conveyors
* Separators
* Coolers

The architecture is intentionally designed to be industry-agnostic and expandable to other industrial sectors.

## Standards Alignment

ARIP is designed with alignment to major industrial and reliability standards, including:

* ISO 14224
* ISO 55000
* ISO 31000
* IEC 62443
* ISA-95
* OPC UA
