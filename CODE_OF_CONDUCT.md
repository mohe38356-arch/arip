# ARIP Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

ARIP is an open-source industrial software initiative focused on reliability intelligence, condition monitoring, asset intelligence, digital twins, industrial AI, and autonomous maintenance.

We are committed to creating a respectful, professional, inclusive, and technically productive environment for all contributors.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

* Communicate respectfully and professionally
* Provide constructive technical feedback
* Welcome contributors with different levels of experience
* Focus discussions on engineering, research, documentation, and project improvement
* Respect different industrial, academic, and technical backgrounds
* Be patient with new contributors
* Avoid sharing confidential or sensitive industrial data

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

* Harassment, insults, threats, or personal attacks
* Discriminatory language or conduct
* Public or private intimidation
* Publishing private information without permission
* Sharing confidential plant, company, customer, or equipment data
* Deliberate misinformation about industrial safety, reliability, or cybersecurity
* Spam, trolling, or repeated off-topic disruption

---

## Industrial Safety and Data Responsibility

ARIP may include industrial reliability, maintenance, condition monitoring, and asset management topics.

Contributors must avoid publishing:

* Confidential industrial data
* Private company documents
* Security-sensitive OT/ICS information
* Real plant credentials
* Proprietary vendor manuals without permission
* Safety-critical recommendations without proper context

Technical contributions should clearly distinguish between:

* General engineering guidance
* Example data
* Research concepts
* Production-ready recommendations

---

## Reporting Issues

If you notice behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it through the repository issue tracker or by contacting the project maintainer.

Reports should include:

* A clear description of the issue
* Relevant links or screenshots, if available
* Any context needed to understand the situation

All reports will be reviewed fairly and respectfully.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take appropriate action, including:

* Clarifying expectations
* Removing inappropriate content
* Closing disruptive issues or discussions
* Restricting participation in the project when necessary

The goal of enforcement is to protect the quality, safety, and professionalism of the ARIP open-source community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all ARIP project spaces, including:

* GitHub issues
* Pull requests
* Discussions
* Documentation
* Community communication channels
* Related project events or collaborations
